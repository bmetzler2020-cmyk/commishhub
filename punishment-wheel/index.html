Hall of Shame

The punishments your league will actually remember.

Waffle House

Twenty-four hours inside a Waffle House is not a punishment — it's a character study. You will see things. You will hear things. Every waffle eaten removes one hour from your sentence, which means you will eat a lot of waffles, and you will not enjoy a single one. The group chat gets updates.

The Waxing

There is no clause in the rulebook that protects you from this. Full body wax, documented, shared with the league. Some leagues have done this once and never finished last again. The deterrent effect alone makes it worth keeping in rotation every year.

Full Autopick

You spent hours on mock drafts. You had a board. You had a strategy. None of it matters — the algorithm gets the wheel next season and you get whatever it decides. This punishment hits hardest for the person who takes fantasy football the most seriously, which is usually the person who finished last.

The Essay

Five hundred words. Written. Printed. Read aloud at the draft party in front of everyone who watched you lose. The essay must explain, in detail, what went wrong and why. No blaming injuries. No blaming the algorithm. The jokes are optional but the accountability is not.

The Sign

One hour. A busy intersection. A sign that reads "I finished last in fantasy football." The sign must be legible from a moving vehicle. Photo evidence is required every fifteen minutes. Leagues that have done this report that the loser waves to approximately forty people, and about half of them honk in support.

The Honorary Bust

The player who hurt you most this season — the one you drafted too early, trusted too long, kept on your roster through three straight goose eggs — now has a namesake. Honorary only. Not legally binding. But the league will remember the name, and so will you, every time someone brings it up at the draft for the next five years.
